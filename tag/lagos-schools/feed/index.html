THE PARTY OF THE YEAR! WHAT IS THE HYPE ALL ABOUT?

We called it the Y2k Extravaganza…

Editor:

Shoyinka Ayomipe (Head Girl and member of the Press Club 2023/24 session)

It is truly an honor to write this article on the party of the year hosted by UPSS Schools. It was no doubt one of the best school parties of my life. Many describe it as the party of the year just because it was packed with fun-filled events like never before. There is no denying that we all grew up amidst old-school fashion, hence the reason for assigning a theme to the party. We called it the Y2K extravaganza, as both students and teachers showed up in their various Y2K fashions.

However, what good is dressing up if it is not fun? But this was not the case at all. I am talking of interactive games, fun games, funny games, and even messy games, all with the inclusion of our outstanding teachers. It was indeed a lot of fun. My favorite of all the activities was when we played the Mr. and Mrs. Pacific round of games. It was no surprise that the senior class claimed the crown. Have you ever had to win something of very little value but still be thrilled about it? Well, I have. Imagine hundreds of people, our teachers included, all bidding to win different sessions of ₦500 airtime recharged cards. Crazy right?

The most exciting part came when it was time to bust a move on the dance floor of the Dance! Dance! Dance! Segment. If I may ask, how many of you can still remember those old dance moves such as Azonto, Skelewu, or even our old songs such as Ema dami duro by OBO, if you see me get swag by Wizkid, the Obobo by Olamide, and a host of others? You can trust that, as students, we did an excellent job. Not to mention the amazing performances put together by the students. There is no doubt that the party was a blast and will continue to remain evergreen in the memory of my fellow students.

After the party was over, you can trust Pacific to provide us with merriments, goodies and souvenirs to take home. Although UPSS Schools have been doing amazingly well in the area of academics, there is no denying that they are also equal to the task when it comes to social activities.

2023 Cultural Day Celebration

2023 Cultural Day Celebration

Culture can be said to be the beliefs, values, behaviour and material objects that constitute a people’s way of life. As a school, we see every need to educate our students about the diverse ethnic groups and their cultural backgrounds in our dear nation. Culture can be likened to the umbilical cord that connects every man to his root, hence the need to intimate our students about their cultural backgrounds with this remarkable event.

This year’s cultural celebration captures strategic indigenous ethnic groups like the Igbo, Hausa, Yoruba, and Edo/Delta cultural groups. To promote global inclusiveness, this year’s celebration transcends our national boundaries to explore Indian culture. The students were painstakingly groomed to showcase the aforementioned cultures in terms of their native attire, language, belief system, and local delicacies. It was a sight to behold as the students independently presented news in their local dialects and showcased their culinary skills as they took us through the process of cooking various tribal delicacies and glamorous fashion parades that portray different tribes within and outside Nigeria.

The celebration was heightened by a good drama presentation that reinforced the need for unity, irrespective of our different ethnicities, to enhance our collective growth as a nation. Also, parents couldn’t help but dance along in succession with the presentation of the Yoruba, Igbo and Edo cultural dances. Of course, the event was not devoid of competitiveness, as the Indian cultural group took centre stage with their groundbreaking performance in their colorful costumes.

The celebration climaxed with a buffet as parents, teachers, students and all guests made their choices from the variety of local delicacies that cut across all tribes and cultures. The photo session and videos are available for your viewing pleasure. It is indeed an event to look forward to.

Evevakpor, Bishop Efe

Cultural Day coordinator.

Biennial Inter-House Sports Competition 2023 Edition.

BIENNIAL INTER-HOUSE SPORTS COMPETITION 2023

The 2023 Inter-house Sports Competition was held on Saturday, 18th February 2023, at the Nigerian Air Force Base Sports Ground, Shasha, Akowonjo, Lagos.

It was an activity-filled day. The air of excitement was eminent as the students were in high spirits and full of expectations.

Sports is an essential part of our school’s co-curricular activities. It caters for the physical well-being of the students. Through the various sporting activities in the school, many of our students have discovered their passion and talent.

It was an opportunity for our students to showcase their talents and skills in various sporting activities.

The principal, Mrs. Ogunsola, in her welcome address, highlighted some of the importance of sports for students:

•  Sport helps to achieve discipline.

•  It fosters respect and friendship.

•  It encourages resilience and teamwork.

• It is a strong uniting force that binds people together irrespective of religion or ethnicity.

In every competition, there must be a winner. At the end of the event, Blue house emerged as the overall winner with a total of 13 gold medals, 6 silver medals, and 6 bronze medals; Red House came second with 12 gold medals, 9 silver medals, and 3 bronze medals; Yellow house came third with 3 gold medals, 8 silver medals, and 5 bronze medals.
