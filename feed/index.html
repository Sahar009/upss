UPSS Schools Valedictory Service 2024: Celebration of achievement and future aspirations

UPSS Schools Valedictory Service 2024: Celebration of Achievement and Future Aspirations

At the multipurpose hall of the school, the UPSS Schools community assembled on Saturday, July 27th, 2024, for an amazing valedictory service. It was a time when the efforts, commitment, and success of the graduands were warmly celebrated with appreciation from family, teachers, and friends.

The graduation ceremony kicked off when the graduands, led by members of the Divisional Boys Scout team, made a grand entrance into the hall. All present erupted into a round of applause and pride as they made their procession to give the day its sombre tone.

The atmosphere was even further taken to greater heights by the praise and worship session. Parents, staff, and students danced and sang unrestrainedly, their hearts full of praise and joy on having been able to cross such a landmark.

The Principal of UPSS Comprehensive College, Mrs. Chioma Ogunsola, in her welcome address, showed deep appreciation to the Chairman of the Board of Trustees, Mr. Omosowon Idowu Remigious, for his immense support and encouragement. She pinpointed integrity as one of the most important values of the school, going ahead to emphasize how the institution had always preached success through hard work, never by short cuts. This, among other precepts, she said, had been deeply installed in the hearts of the students. She also acknowledged the graduands, many of whom had been in the care of the UPSS Schools family since nursery school days. She reminded them that the invaluable lessons acquired within the walls of UPSS Comprehensive College would stand them in good stead in the years to come.

Watch full event here

Life will throw challenges at you,” she said, “but it is how you respond to the opportunities, the time, and the challenges that life presents that will ultimately shape your destination.” She urged the students to embrace courage, face obstacles head-on, and never take failure as an option. The Headteacher of the Nursery and Primary School resonated with these sentiments in her address, warmly welcoming the audience and congratulating the youngest graduands on their remarkable achievements. Her words brimmed with encouragement, urging the pupils to carry forth the values they had learned as they embarked on the next stage of their education.

The event was marked with a motivational speech delivered by the guest speaker, Omooba (Barr.) Taiwo Gbenga Owokalade.

His speech entitled “The Power to Dream” proved both inspiring and evocative. Drawing on the wisdom of some of the world’s great leaders like Eleanor Roosevelt, Peter Drucker, Abraham Lincoln, Harriet Tubman, and Martin Luther King Jr., he underlined the role of dreams, the surmounting of defeat, and ongoing movement. 

The audience, especially the prospective graduates, found solace and inspiration in his candid sharing of personal experiences and life lessons, as they prepared to navigate their own paths. 

Next up, the College Head Prefects took to the stage; these included the Head Boy and Head Girl. Their emotion-filled and appreciative words were met with thunderous applause from both their peers and the audience. The farewells from the prefects of the Primary and Nursery Schools were equally moving, as they each looked back at their different experiences and the relationships built over the years. 

Pastor Dr. Gbenga Odubanjo gracefully led the spiritual dimension of the ceremony and raised his voice in prayer for the graduands, appealing for guidance, protection, and success in all their future activities. The event featured colourful performances by the students, both in music and dance routines, which thrilled the guests and provided a happy ending to the formal proceedings.

 

The Chairman, Mr. Omosowon Idowu Remigious, then stepped onto the stage to deliver an address entitled “Charting a Path to Success for Relevance and Impact.” His speech emphasized the importance of hard work, dedication, and strategic planning as essential components for future success, leaving a profound impression on everyone present. As the event came to a close, distinguished students from various subjects and departments received accolades, even for non-academic achievements. These were in confirmation of an all-round education that UPSS Schools tries to instill. The valedictory service of 2024 came to an end with a lively photoshoot session, immortalizing the joy and pride radiating from the graduates, their families, and the dedicated school staff. As the ceremony drew to a graceful close, the Principal of the Sixth Form College extended a heartfelt vote of thanks. The unfolding day was a day of celebrating the past, of reflecting on the present, of an optimistic look toward the future in which the graduates of UPSS Schools were ready to go into the next phase of their lives with courage and confidence.

2023 Cultural Day Celebration

2023 Cultural Day Celebration

Culture can be said to be the beliefs, values, behaviour and material objects that constitute a people’s way of life. As a school, we see every need to educate our students about the diverse ethnic groups and their cultural backgrounds in our dear nation. Culture can be likened to the umbilical cord that connects every man to his root, hence the need to intimate our students about their cultural backgrounds with this remarkable event.

This year’s cultural celebration captures strategic indigenous ethnic groups like the Igbo, Hausa, Yoruba, and Edo/Delta cultural groups. To promote global inclusiveness, this year’s celebration transcends our national boundaries to explore Indian culture. The students were painstakingly groomed to showcase the aforementioned cultures in terms of their native attire, language, belief system, and local delicacies. It was a sight to behold as the students independently presented news in their local dialects and showcased their culinary skills as they took us through the process of cooking various tribal delicacies and glamorous fashion parades that portray different tribes within and outside Nigeria.

The celebration was heightened by a good drama presentation that reinforced the need for unity, irrespective of our different ethnicities, to enhance our collective growth as a nation. Also, parents couldn’t help but dance along in succession with the presentation of the Yoruba, Igbo and Edo cultural dances. Of course, the event was not devoid of competitiveness, as the Indian cultural group took centre stage with their groundbreaking performance in their colorful costumes.

The celebration climaxed with a buffet as parents, teachers, students and all guests made their choices from the variety of local delicacies that cut across all tribes and cultures. The photo session and videos are available for your viewing pleasure. It is indeed an event to look forward to.

Evevakpor, Bishop Efe

Cultural Day coordinator.

Biennial Inter-House Sports Competition 2023 Edition.

BIENNIAL INTER-HOUSE SPORTS COMPETITION 2023

The 2023 Inter-house Sports Competition was held on Saturday, 18th February 2023, at the Nigerian Air Force Base Sports Ground, Shasha, Akowonjo, Lagos.

It was an activity-filled day. The air of excitement was eminent as the students were in high spirits and full of expectations.

Sports is an essential part of our school’s co-curricular activities. It caters for the physical well-being of the students. Through the various sporting activities in the school, many of our students have discovered their passion and talent.

It was an opportunity for our students to showcase their talents and skills in various sporting activities.

The principal, Mrs. Ogunsola, in her welcome address, highlighted some of the importance of sports for students:

•  Sport helps to achieve discipline.

•  It fosters respect and friendship.

•  It encourages resilience and teamwork.

• It is a strong uniting force that binds people together irrespective of religion or ethnicity.

In every competition, there must be a winner. At the end of the event, Blue house emerged as the overall winner with a total of 13 gold medals, 6 silver medals, and 6 bronze medals; Red House came second with 12 gold medals, 9 silver medals, and 3 bronze medals; Yellow house came third with 3 gold medals, 8 silver medals, and 5 bronze medals.

Valedictory Service 2022 At UPSS Schools

Valedictory Service 2022 at UPSS Comprehensive College

The 2022 valedictory service of UPSS Comprehensive College took place on Saturday, 2nd July, 2022. It was a day of joy and celebration as parents, grandparents, uncles, aunties and other relatives gathered at the School Multipurpose Hall to celebrate their children/wards as they bade good bye to secondary education.

The principal, Mrs. Ogunsola, in her address, urged the graduands to believe in themselves and shun examination malpractice as they progress in their educational career, thereby upholding the good image of the school.

The chairman, board of trustees of UPSS Schools, Mr. Remigious Omosowon, in his speech, titled ‘‘Grit Matters More than Talent’’ enumerated the importance of Grit to the graduands, stating that Grit is important because it is a driver of achievement and success, independent of and beyond what talent and intelligence contribute.

He also encouraged the graduands to build their Grit by clarifying their goals, discovering their interests, knowing their purpose and practicing optimistic self-talk (making affirmations)

The guest speaker for the day, Engr. Demola Olorunfemi, Chief Lecturer, Department of Agric. Engineering, Lagos State Polytechnic, in his speech, titled ‘‘Career Choice and Innovation’’, advised the graduands to consider their academic strengths, personal qualities and social skills when choosing a career.

Out of the forty-one students in the class of 2022, the head boy of the School, Adeleke Habeebullah, emerged as the Valedictorian with nine A1s in his final examination.

Congratulations to UPSS Schools and the class of 2022.
